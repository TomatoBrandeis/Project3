Sketch for 3d Printing

Sketch used for the print

This is the sketch and measurements of the radio that I had to solder in order to create a cover for using the 3d printer.

Fitting the Radio

How the radio fits in the 3d print

This is showing my idea of having the 3d printed cover connect like legos and using friction to keep it on. The design sadly did not work as the hole was too tight and when I tried to pull it apart again, it broke off a corner peg of the piece. Next time, I would give it at least 0.2-0.3 mm more room to fit the pieces or create a lock design in the 3d printed version to connect them.

3D Printed

Cover for Radio

This is the front cover on top of the radio (with knob) of the 3d printed outcome of my measurements. When I put the measurements inside of fusion and designed with the component measurements, I shifted the buttons (too far left) and the knob could fit, but it was a very tight fight with a lot of friction. The back piece of the radio matched perfectly and the battery was able to go through, but my design for the cover was to have the pieces split into 2 and connect like legos, but the holes were a bit too small and misaligned so the box couldn't be fit together.

Laser Cut

Laser Cut Cover for Radio

This is the look of the laser cut version of the cover for the radio. The hole for the knob and buttons are the right size, but they are off by a tiny but so they do not fit the radio properly which is why it wasn't added within the case. The design was meant to have lock-joints, but the conversion from 360 to lightburn did not work, so I will have to create another box with correct (or larger) dimensions and positions for the knob and buttons, as well as add in the lock joints in order to create a proper container for the radio.
